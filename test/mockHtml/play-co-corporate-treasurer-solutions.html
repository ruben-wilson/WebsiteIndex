Value for Clients
SWPM
Swap Manager
Primary benchmarking and valuation tool for interest rate derivatives. Used to model swaps and loans, determine mid market, calculate fair value, greeks, accrued interest and expected cashflows.
OVML
Option Pricer FX/Commodity
Benchmarking and valuation tool for FX and commodity derivatives. Used to model linear and non-linear structures and risk reversals. Occasional use for exotics such as knock-ins, knock-outs and digital options. Offers measurement of fair-value, accrued, greeks and scenario analysis.
MARS
Multi-Asset Risk System
A risk agregration tool, allowing for capturing of exposure and hedges under the same framework of analysis for the purposes of calculation of contemporary or historic fair-value and different measures of risk, such as greeks, value-at-risk" (VAR), scenario analysis, credit risk, CVA/DVA adjustments (XVA) and also offers a collateral management tool (BCOL). The users can leverage either Bloomberg's GUI or visualize the risk in third party tools leveraging MARS API.
FXGO
FX Electronic Trading
FX market place/trading platform, allowing for an integrated electronic FX markets and execute FX spot, forwards, options, NDFs and place cash deposits. This tool and its components play a very important role in the fully automated front-to-bank FX workflow. It is the preferred tool by market participants in the NDF markets.
HEFF
Multi-Asset Risk System: Hedge Accounting
Hedge effectiveness testing tool, which allows users to determine, test and document if a hedge or hedging strategy can qualify for the application of hedge-accounting treatement under different accounting regimes: IAS39, FAS, FAS-U, IFRS9 etc. The tool communicates directly with SWPM and OVML, thus allowing for testing of interest rates, FX and commodity strategies.

Enterprise Data

Solutions
Value for Clients
Collateral Tagging (bank treasury)
Treasurers need to be able to identify what securities they are able to post as collateral for non-centrally cleared OTC derivatives. This is especially important as corporate treasurers who may need to collatoralize transactions with counterparties such as repo or loans.
HQLA (bank treasury)
Understanding whether the collateral or assets they hold are High Quality Liquid Instruments. A corporate treasurer would feed this into their internal system and we need to view HQLA for regulatory purposes
Liquidity
Understanding the liquidity of instruments or assets the corporate is holding.
Default Risk
Corporate treasurers will be interested in looking at default risk on themselves as this is what banks will be looking at when the look to help finance a company. They will also be looking at default risk of companies they are also involved in inter-company loans and transfer pricing.
Curves
Interest rate and FX curves, feed curves in treasury management system to calculate cash flows and value of their portfolio.
Volatility
If a corporate treasury hedges with options, they may need to feed volatilities into their treasury management system for valuations.
Pricing
Corporate treasurers will need to understad the prices of loans they have and also any debt they have issued. For this, they will need good quality pricing sources such as BVAL, CBBT, BGN. This will also need quality pricing for repo instruments where MSG1 data would be useful. They also need a good quality pricing source for valuing their assets across cash and OTC.
