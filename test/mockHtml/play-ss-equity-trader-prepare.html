Context

2023 was largely a good year for equity with global indices up 20 percent. Volatility is anticipated in equities in 2024 as asset allocation tilts to fixed incomeI on the back of expected rates cuts and uncertainty from economic slowing, geopolitics, and company fundamentals.

While fast moving markets can certainly create opportunity, often people can get caught in unfavorable situations. In most equity trading situations, you have three different options for exectution: high-touch, low-touch, and dark pools. High-touch trading is where humans are involved and they execute trades themselves. Low-touch refers to any algorithmic trading.

Lastly, dark pool trades occur off of the exchange between two parties with the transaction only being reported 90 seconds after it has occured. These are often used for larger orders to disguise the size and obtain better pricing.

All stages of trading workflow are undergoing streamlining and cost-reduction: pre-trade, execution and post-trade. Rising demand for smart intelligence gathering and processing requires new tech in a world of almost limitless data. Auto-booking from chat-scraping technology is improving booking efficiency at larger banks. Sell sides are moving away from manual execution and ticketing to automated processes and pricing.

Requirements for smarter and faster execution has led to huge growth in algos for market making, flow execution, and the hedging of risk. Straight-through processing (STP) from chat to settlement is reducing operational risk in the booking workflow. The challenge is greater for small banks which typically have less STP and automation in general, which increases operational risk.

When trading a stock you can be faced with favorable and unfavorable momentum. When executing a large order, there can be situations where a trader needs to understand different factors, like the participation rate and duration. A trade cost model, such as TCA <GO>, can help a trader gauge how much their order will move the market. If the trade takes longer to fully execute, there is a risk the market can move against you.

Trade cost models can give different participation rates and the cost of that total trade. They also give the cost of going to the market with that block of stock and what that will cost you if there is no-one on the other side of the trade. Bloomberg's transaction cost analysis tools can help. This is the other side of the coin to algo and automated execution.

Connect

2024 is the year of “more”: more elections, more news, more uncertainty. Known client challenges include too much information and too little time, difficulty cutting through noise and generating meaningful insight quickly, consistency of data and putting data into context, and discoverability of content due to fragmentation of technology.

Clients face the challenge of deciding whether falling inflation suggests a “soft landing” for global economies, or a recession; Central Bank pivots will cause volatility as market participants try to anticipate timing of cuts.

And with more elections than ever before in 2024 and outcomes harder to call, customers face difficulty monitoring opinion and modelling outcomes. New risks can accelerate quickly. Finding trusted data and staying close to predictions and opinions is challenging.

The rally in equity markets has not lifted all stocks equally and there are now wide variations in valuations across sectors and regions. Clients will need to know where the big outliers are now and how to dig deeper into specific stocks they are screening.

As the US begins to cut rates, there could be potential capital movement from USD to undervalued currencies, causing them to outperform USD. Due to the rate cutting environment and geopolitical events, FX volatility will increase.

Economists, credit investors and equity investors offer conflicting outlooks for 2024, making equity forecasts particularly difficult. Clients need to understand different perspectives on the economy and their impact on the investable/ tradable universe.
