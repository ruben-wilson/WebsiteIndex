Jan 2024 Edition

Build the Narrative

Identify trends and client challenges to open doors to sales opportunities. Learn about the competitive landscape, questions to ask and what solutions we can offer.

Context

Interest rates are starting to have an impact on the wider economy. Economic slowdown and predicted mild recession may help lower inflation further. Monetary policy reversal is initiating some rebound. Some 50% of the world’s population are facing elections in a a polarized political landscape, with >2 billion votes across 50 countries (half the global economy).

Political (in)stability is more troubling than it was 12 months ago. The US election is impossible to call, so we can expect market volatility.

All stages of trading workflow are undergoing streamlining and cost-reduction: pre-trade, execution and post-trade. Rising demand for smart intelligence gathering and processing requires new tech in a world of almost limitless data. Auto-booking from chat-scraping technology is improving booking efficiency at larger banks.

Sell sides are moving away from manual execution and ticketing to automated processes and pricing. Requirements for smarter and faster execution has led to huge growth in algorithms for market making, flow execution, and hedging of risk.

Banks want to understand more about their trades and clients. This includes hit-miss ratios when pitching trade ideas and quotes, and intelligence on which client are best to speak to when you have particular inventory to move.

Traders typically already know their biggest clients well. It’s their small clients they know less. This information can help them more effectively identify smaller clients they may want to deal more with to grow their overall business.

Clients are looking for more streamlined technology stacks to reduce data and system redundancy by getting systems to speak with each other and share information. This can be accelerated via the Cloud.

Auto-booking from chat scraping technology is improving booking efficiency at larger banks. Straight-through Processing (STP) from chat to settlement is reducing operational risk in the booking workflow. The challenge is greater for smaller banks which typically have less STP and automation in general which increases operational risk.

Connect

Clients face the challenge of deciding whether falling inflation suggests a “soft landing” for global economies, or a recession. Central Bank pivots will cause volatility as market participants try to anticipate timing of cuts. And with more elections than ever before in 2024 and outcomes harder to call, customers face difficulty monitoring opinion and modelling outcomes. New risks can accelerate quickly. Finding trusted data and staying close to predictions and opinions is challenging.

2024’s expected cut in central bank rates will likely create a trading tilt, i.e. everyone will want to be long bonds. Any pick-up in defaults or credit downgrades could see spreads widen so issuer selection and liquidity will become more important.

With Covid-19 era cheaply-issued 3–5-year debt approaching refinancing timelines, borrowers soon face higher funding costs due to the current rate environment. Primary market participants must monitor how deals are priced in a fast-changing environment.

Credit spreads are currently historically tight, but market sentiment is expecting credit default rates to rise due to the macro environment. Evaluating relative value across sectors and issuers is increasingly important.

Terminal Key Selling Points
  1. Widely used tools for distributing runs and liquidiity information across the primary and secondarty markets, along with electronic trading.
  2. Security-level bond data including reference data, holdings, independent pricing, liquidity and risk analytics, and relative value.
  3. Integration of market data with OMS.
