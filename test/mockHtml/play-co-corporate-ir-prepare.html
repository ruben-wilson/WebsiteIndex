Build the Narrative

Identify trends and client challenges to open doors to sales opportunities. Learn about the competitive landscape, questions to ask and what solutions we can offer.

Context

Interest rates are starting to have an impact on the wider economy. Economic slowdown and predicted mild recession may help lower inflation further. Monetary policy reversal is initiating some rebound.

China's re-opening and economic stats in 2023 failed to meet market expectations. Its ailing real estate sector is sprawling into 2024, causing wide concern over deflation, unemployment and the success of economic restructuring; the spillover to other parts of the world is now a shared concern among global players.

Central bank tightening has pushed marginal funding costs for businesses sharply higher over the past couple of years. If interest rates remain high, companies will need to devote a greater share of their revenue to cover higher interest expense as they refinance their debt at higher rates.

It will be challenging for companies to maintain revenues and margins in the face of a slowing economy, especially combined with increased refinancing costs. Any deterioration in the economic situation and/or key credit ratios could result in widening of spreads or potentially an increase in defaults.

The changing nature of the shareholding base. What was once a predominantly active, long-only shareholder base is now a mixture of passive ETFs, long-short hedge funds, activist investors, and long-only investors spread around the world.

It has never been more challenging for a company to monitor these changes, understand the investment strategy of shareholders, and structure a strategy to mould that base into a stable foundation.

Maintaining analyst coverage is a growing issue. Cost and efficiency pressures on sell side research is forcing a reduction in analyst coverage, along with the departure of many senior analysts from the industry.

As a result, institutional and other investor interest in a company can fall and share valuation consquentially suffer. Helping IROs nurture sell-side relationships, and communicate with them effectively is important.

Demands for accurate and detailed ESG and sustainability disclosures by companies are only likely to increase in many parts of the world. Understanding requirements, industry best practices, and the data available to them will allow IROs to structure effective communication and reporting of the goals their company puts in place. Peer comparision is also important to track progress.

The ESG sector has faced accusations of greenwashing, which has resulted in greater scrutiny of the adherence to, and validity of, investment policies and financing activities, and a focus on credibility of corporate commitment.

Connect

Clients face the challenge of deciding whether falling inflation suggests a “soft landing” for global economies, or a recession. Central Bank pivots will cause volatility as market participants try to anticipate timing of cuts.

And with more elections than ever before in 2024 and outcomes harder to call, customers face difficulty monitoring opinion and modelling outcomes. New risks can accelerate quickly. Finding trusted data and staying close to predictions and opinions is challenging.

Investors bemoan a lack of available data, along with transparency and consistency. There is an increasing burden on all market participants on disclosure requirements at an entity or product level. Climate risk involves assessing the impact of a company on the climate, and understanding the impact of the changing climate on the company.

It needs to be integrated into broad risk management workflows, and requires complex forward-looking assessment. Although financial impact is hard to quantify, it is still required by regulators. Lack of transparency and data gaps are common and often filled with opaque estimates or assumptions.

With Covid-19 era cheaply-issued 3–5-year debt approaching refinancing timelines, borrowers soon face higher funding costs due to the current rate environment. Primary market participants must monitor how deals are priced in a fast-changing environment.

Credit spreads are currently historically tight, but market sentiment is expecting credit default rates to rise due to the macro environment. Evaluating relative value across sectors and issuers is increasingly important.
