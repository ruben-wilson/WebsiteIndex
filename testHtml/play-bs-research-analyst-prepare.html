Build the Narrative

Identify trends and client challenges to open doors to sales opportunities. Learn about the competitive landscape, questions to ask and what solutions we can offer.

Context

Emerging technologies, such as AI and Natural Language Processing, are playing a larger role in helping buy sides facing an ever growing data and issuer universe. They can facilitate idea generation, mine text and improve production of proprietary research. Such innovations can elevate efficient analysis of both internal and external sell-side research.

Sourcing, cleansing and managing dependable data is the real challenge as an input factor into financial modelling. Quant analysts have billions of potential data points from hundreds of sources at their fingertips.

Interest rates are starting to have an impact on the wider economy. Economic slowdown and predicted mild recession may help lower inflation further. Monetary policy reversal is initiating some rebound. Some 50% of the world’s population are facing elections in a polarised political landscape, with >2 billion votes across 50 countries (half the global economy).

Political (in)stability is more troubling than it was 12 months ago. The US election is impossible to call, so we can expect volatility.

ESG has become a mainstream strategy, both due to investor demand and regulation on sustainable investing. Despite some headwinds, it has continued to attract AUM and the number of financial institutions that integrate ESG in their decision-making is on the rise globally.

Larger buy sides now often have a specialist ESG or sustainability research team to determine scoring and produce thematic work for ordinary analysts and portfolio managers to incorporate into their work.

Re-opening and economic stats in 2023 failed to meet market expectations; the ailing real estate sector is sprawling into 2024, causing wide concern over deflation, unemployment and the success of economic restructuring.

The spillover to other parts of the world is now a shared concern among global players.

With global indebtedness continung to grow and issuers from emerging regions increasingly accessing hard currency funding, the debt universe is growing. In addition, more and more data is being published, including alternative and green bond data.

Consuming this and analyzing the issuer landscape as well as the issuer profiles becomes ever more challenging against growing demands to give opinion and produce timely research.

Connect

2024 is the year of “more”: more elections, more news, more uncertainty. Known client challenges include too much information and too little time, difficulty cutting through noise and generating meaningful insight quickly, consistency of data and putting data into context, and discoverability of content due to fragmentation of technology.

Clients face the challenge of deciding whether falling inflation suggests a “soft landing” for global economies, or a recession; Central Bank pivots will cause volatility as market participants try to anticipate timing of cuts.

And with more elections than ever before in 2024 and outcomes harder to call, customers face difficulty monitoring opinion and modelling outcomes. New risks can accelerate quickly. Finding trusted data and staying close to predictions and opinions is challenging.

The rally in equity markets has not lifted all stocks equally and there are now wide variations in valuations across sectors and regions. Clients will need to know where the big outliers are now and how to dig deeper into specific stocks they are screening.

Credit spreads are currently historically tight, but market sentiment is expecting credit default rates to rise due to the macro environment. Evaluating relative value across sectors and issuers is increasingly important. Sharp moves in government bonds have left some markets distorted. Tracking and explaining these dislocations is also important.

As the US begins to cut rates, there could be potential capital movement from USD to undervalued currencies, causing them to outperform USD. Due to the rate cutting environment and geopolitical events, FX volatility will increase.

Economists, credit investors and equity investors offer conflicting outlooks for 2024, making equity forecasts particularly difficult. Clients need to understand different perspectives on the economy and their impact on the investable/ tradable universe.
