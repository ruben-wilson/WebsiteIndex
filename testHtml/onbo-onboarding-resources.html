Matthew Rees

Global Head of Corporate Sales

@Bloomberg since September 2000

Past roles you've held

Sales Manager for Northern Europe, Asia Head of Terminal Sales, Americas Head of Terminal Sales, Global Head of Sales for Enterprise Data, Global Head of Sales for Electronic Trading (plus removal man and barman).

Bloomberg offices you’ve been based in

London, Singapore, Tokyo and New York.

Where you're from

Welsh blood but born and raised in London.

Proudest Bloomberg accomplishment to date

  • Helping Bloomberg exceed 300,000 Terminals
  • Being part of the team that helped Bloomberg's data business exceed $1bn in revenue
  • Being part of the team that launched our Global Rate Card for our Fixed Income Electronic Trading Business

A colleague who has made a positive impact on your career

Peter Grauer has inspired, mentored and encouraged me at some crucial moments in my career at Bloomberg.

The most interesting place a client visit has taken you

The Peruvian president's office at the Government Palace of Peru in Lima.

What you can not go a day without

A few squares of Green & Black's chocolate.

One moment where you thought "That’s so Bloomberg"

In 2011, I was working in our Tokyo office. After the 2011 Tohoku earthquake and tsunami, in addition to the shock of those events, there was a great deal of concern about the risk of radiation exposure in Tokyo as a result of the Fukushima nuclear disaster. Bloomberg as a company made extraordinary efforts to support our team in Japan, up to and including making evacuations arrangements for employees and their families and opening temporary offices and accommodations in other parts of the country. The people involved in that response ranged from members of the Management Committee to our security team to Japanese-speaking employees from all departments in other offices around the globe. It was a remarkable example of coming together to help our colleagues and communities and was truly the “Best of Bloomberg.”

FON Matthew Rees

Bing Li

Head of APAC Core Sales

@Bloomberg since June 2017

Past roles you've held

I’m currently the head of Greater China at Bloomberg. Prior to joining Bloomberg, I was head of Fixed Income at Bank of China (Hong Kong), responsible for sales, trading and research. I joined the bank from Tandem Global Partners, a New York-based fixed income asset manager, where he was a partner and portfolio manager covering the Asia Pacific region. Before Tandem, I was a VP at Deutsche Bank in Hong Kong and member of the Equity Derivatives department. I also worked for HSBC and J.P. Morgan in the early years of my career, where I traded bonds and fixed income derivatives.

Bloomberg offices you’ve been based in

Beijing, Shanghai, Shenzhen and Hong Kong.

Where you're from

Born in Beijing, grew up in New York.

Proudest Bloomberg accomplishment to date

A 200-terminal deal the team delivered this year with the second largest investment bank in China -- the fourth-largest terminal sale in the firm's history. It wasn't so much the size of the deal as it was the team's ambition and willingness to take a risk that made me most proud. They saw a bigger opportunity, came together, asked for what they needed, challenged our ways of working and made it happen. It went from a 100-terminal sale to a 200-terminal sale. To me, that's actionable ambition in a nutshell!

A colleague who has made a positive impact on your career

Jeff Leckstein -- no question, hands down! Organisations can say they are collaborative or inclusive, but when I first joined Bloomberg in 2017, Jeff lived it. He was one of the most senior people in APAC at the time and there was no upside for him -- he just decided to help. He went out of his way to welcome me and dedicated time every week to teach me the “tricks” of the Terminal and how to be the most efficient and effective with my time. It really was quite incredible. His authenticity and care was palpable. It set me up for the role I am in today.

The most interesting place a client visit has taken you

Harbin, the northernmost city in China. It was in my second year with Bloomberg and we were closing a big deal with the CEO of a major automaker there. It was January, so the temperature was -30°C. The client's heating was out, so sitting in the boardroom closing the deal was -10°C. It was an amazing conversation but due to the cold, my eyes were streaming close-to-frozen tears. The client kept asking if I was OK!

What you can not go a day without

A 5:30 a.m. walk with my three dogs -- two Welsh Corgis and one Shiba Inu. It's the quietest 30-40 minutes of the day and it clears my mind -- much more productive than coffee!

One moment where you thought "That’s so Bloomberg"

One of the biggest projects we delivered in 2021 was Southbound Bond Connect. To bring this to fruition, we needed to overcome extreme obstacles in a very, very short timeframe -- even the client thought so. However, we made the case and the ask to the Management Committee. They decided in under 30 minutes to take on the challenge and that this MUST happen. It was the craziest month I've had at Bloomberg so far. But we achieved it. That a 40-year-old company still has the ability to move that fast and implement to such a high standard is amazing and impressive - and “so Bloomberg!”

FON Bing Li
