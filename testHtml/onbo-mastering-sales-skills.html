TSM: The Selling Mindset

Description

TSM is a 5-hour in-person training delivered over 1 day. (APAC via Zoom). The focus is on helping participants unlock their sales potential in the transition from Analytics to Sales. The course will explore the shift in mindset required to be effective in a Sales environment where there is increased autonomy and responsibility. We will look at practical ways you can prepare for this move.

You will also hear first-hand from experienced Salespeople about the kind of career pathway and opportunities you can get from the role. Roleplays will be involved, and each participant will receive peer feedback during the training to help them improve.

Eligibility

Participants must have completed FT1, FT2, call and visit certification. By invitation from an Analytics Manager only.

Pre-work

None

At the end of the training, you will be able to:
  • Explain mindset shift required when moving to sales
  • Describe culture within sales
  • Identify core responsibilities of an Account Manager
  • Recognise qualities of a successful Account Manager
  • Explain how to manage client relationships on a basic level
  • Identify a basic sales strategy
  • Develop your own internal network within Sales

  • Amanda Too & Barry Porter - APAC
  • Alan Ramachandran - EMEA

Agenda

  • The value of sales skills applicable to everyday life

  • Explore career journey in Sales

AM ONBO: Account Manager Onboarding

Description

Sales Onboarding is an in-class course designed to equip you with the practical knowledge and tools to begin your career in Bloomberg’s Enterprise Sales. It covers aspects such as “what is Enterprise Sales?” and how we all work together, what the key elements of the role are, tools to profile clients, analyze pipeline, and monitor your impact. It also includes sections on how to plan a Sales trip, make effective calls and meetings, and manage your diary. Finally it gives exposure to many of the other departments essential to the success of Sales.

Pre-Requisites

We recommend completing the ASSP in-class and e-learning modules before joining. For external hires direct into Sales, this isn’t a prerequisite.

Requirements

Exact structure and delivery differs in the 3 regions. But the core content includes around 25 hours of in-class delivery. Contact the course owners for regional specifics.

GCOM: The Art of Engagement

Description

GCOM is a 10-hour in-person training delivered over 2 days. Participants can expect to learn about a global communication model ranging from perfecting the 'door-opener', good questioning techniques to positioning Bloomberg Unique Value Propositions and going for the close. Being confident, authentic and skillful in the art of engagement is critical to doing this well. Roleplays will be involved and each participant will receive peer feedback during the training to help them improve.

Eligibility

Minimum 6 months experience in Sales (e.g. AM/RM in Care, Sales in Enterprise Data/Products). Employees from other areas of the business who are client-facing may also be eligible.

Pre-work

None

At the end of the training, you will be able to:
  • Explain what motivates people to buy
  • Establish your credibility to get any meeting
  • Experiment using different questions techniques
  • Practice positioning Bloomberg USP
  • Handle client’s objections effectively
  • Ask for client's commitment and close a deal
  • Develop your own genuine and authentic engagement style

BBIZ: Book of Business

Description

BBIZ is a 36 hour in-person training program delivered over 5 days, split by Buy Side and Sell Side. Participants can expect to learn about the workflows of the primary market players, and the enterprise suite of solutions that Bloomberg offers for those players.

At the end of the program, participants are expected to identify and qualify enterprise product and data leads through understanding where Bloomberg fits into the client workflows and what challenges the products solve for our clients. Group presentations will be involved, and participants will receive feedback during the training to help them improve.

Eligibility

Minimum 6 months experience in Sales (e.g. AM or RM in Core, Sales in Enterprise Data Sales/Product). Employees from other areas of the business who are client facing and should be identifying leads in their role may also be eligible. BBIZ builds on Terminal knowledge, therefore participants should have ideally completed an Analytics foundational training or specialist training.

Pre-work

None

At the end of the training, you will be able to:
  • Describe the concept of a value chain
  • Articulate the industry trends that are driving demand for our enterprise suite of solutions
  • Explain on a high level what each enterprise solution is for
  • Describe how each enterprise solution fits into client workflows
  • Ask relevant questions that will discover and qualify sales leads

GEM: The Science of Dealmaking

Description

GEM is an 18-hour in-person training delivered over 3 days. Participants can expect to learn about the six stages of our selling framework - the GEM Model, and the tools required to strategically analyze and progress complex deals with multiple stakeholders. There will be opportunities to practically apply all concepts to your own deal, receiving peer feedback in the process to help them improve.

Eligibility

Minimum 6 months experience in Sales (e.g. RM in Core, Sales in Enterprise Data/Product). Employees from other areas of the business who are managing a complex sales process may also be eligible. Participants must have completed BBIZ.

Pre-work

Participants are required to submit a complex sales deal which they are currently working on, along with relevant high level information to provide context. Participants should be prepared to articulate the deal in class.

At the end of the training, you will be able to:
  • Design unique insights by maintaining an overview of key industry trends and your client's strategic vision
  • Act in a consultative manner to establish early involvement in a customer's buying process
  • Identify Change Agents and coach them to help drive your deal to completion
  • Evaluate stakeholders to identify different types of buyers involved in a deal
  • Align internal stakeholders to form a strong collaborative team to better manage the opportunity
  • Apply the deal assessment framework effectively to control an engagement throughout the entire sales cycle

EOM: Enterprise Operating Model

Description

Enterprise Operating Model (EOM) is a 30-hour in person training delivered over 5 days, split by Buy Side and Sell Side. EOM takes you to the core of your client's business and connects your sales strategy to their enterprise journey. It focuses on the client's strategy and operating model, using tech stacks and user workflows from EOM<GO> to map out People, Process and Technology.

The goal of the program is to give salespeople the knowledge they need to analyze and understand the client's strategy, operating model and technology stack, in order to generate multi-product, multi-year engagements with our most important clients.

Eligibility

For revenue-generating Core TLs, ERMs and Senior RMs with a specific BS/SS focus only. Participants must have completed BBIZ.

Pre-work

Participants are required to read and provide high level analysis of external client case study (1h) and complete their own client survey (15min).

At the end of the training, you will be able to:
  • Identify key internal (customer-specific) and external (industry)_ factors that inform the client’s choice of business strategy.
  • Analyze the chosen customer strategy, articulate and evaluate key priorities for the client.
  • Formulate next steps in order to partner with the customer on their operation model journey.
  • Depict the customer’s tech stack and workflows, illustrating key pain points and areas for strategic development.
  • Broaden your sales engagement with the customer to encompass previously unaddressed parts of the customer’s value chain.
  • Refer with confidence to EOM frameworks when preparing for meetings and engaging clients on complex topics and workflows.
