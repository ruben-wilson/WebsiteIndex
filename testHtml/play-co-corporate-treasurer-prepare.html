Build the Narrative

Identify trends and client challenges to open doors to sales opportunities. Learn about the competitive landscape, questions to ask and what solutions we can offer.

Context

Interest rates are starting to have an impact on the wider economy. Economic slowdown and predicted mild recession may help lower inflation further. Monetary policy reversal is initiating some rebound.

Some 50% of the world’s population are facing elections in a polarised political landscape, with >2 billion votes across 50 countries (half the global economy). Political (in)stability is more troubling than it was 12 months ago. The US election is impossible to call, so we can expect volatility.

The most comprehesive trend noticable across entities and geographies is automation. The global Covid crisis put ongoing discussions about automating and streamlining processes on over-drive.

The flexible working environment moved previously nice-to-have discussions to must-have ones about process improvement and minimization of manual tasks.

Another distinct trend relates to the overall evolution of the Treasury Department and expansion of its duties. For example, Treasury's role is evolving from traditional cash-management to partnering C-suites to achieve strategic firm goals in the areas including enterprise risk managment, optimalization of capital funding, and minimization of cost of capital. All of these result in effective exposure risk management.

The US Treasury's expanding role and internal influence includes commodities. Traditionally commodities have been managed by the Procurement Department.

However, with the increased volatility of commodity prices in recent years and its impact on top line earnings has led to ever increased involvement of the Treasury Department at some firms from a risk oversight perspective.

Traditionally, treasurers hedged a fixed ratio of receivables and payables. But, as corporations become multinational, they may now have multiple treasury centers where FX hedging is performed. Such companies can be more sensitive to the impact of the various increased hedging activities on their consolidated balance sheets.

This raises the importance of hedging optimization, where the impact on the consolidated balance sheet of each individual hedging activity is assessed. As a result, some hedges may not be performed. This is known as earnings-at-risk analysis.

Connect

2024 is the year of “more”: more elections, more news, more uncertainty. Known client challenges include too much information and too little time, difficulty cutting through noise and generating meaningful insight quickly, consistency of data and putting data into context, and discoverability of content due to fragmentation of technology.

Clients face the challenge of deciding whether falling inflation suggests a “soft landing” for global economies, or a recession; Central Bank pivots will cause volatility as market participants try to anticipate timing of cuts.

And with more elections than ever before in 2024 and outcomes harder to call, customers face difficulty monitoring opinion and modelling outcomes. New risks can accelerate quickly. Finding trusted data and staying close to predictions and opinions is challenging.

With markets full of volatility, fast hedging decisions to secure the best prices with efficient execution are key to protecting against downside risk.

Terminal Key Selling Points
  1. Price discovery, plus electronic execution for FX, IRS vanilla swaps, and partially for commodities.
  2. Transparent valuation tools (MARS VAL, OVML, SWPM), which typically require enterprise licenses to be used alongside a Terminal.
  3. As part of an integrated workflow with additional enterprise licenses for internal and external data/valuations, risk reports.
