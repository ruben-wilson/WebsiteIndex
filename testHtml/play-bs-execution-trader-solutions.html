Enterprise Data: Equity

Solutions
Value for Clients
Real-Time Pricing & Volume
Traders can leverage B-PIPE to stream live exchange data into their OMS and trading systems.
New Security Set Up
Traders need to have the new securities set up and mastered in their internal systems as quickly as possible so they can trade.
Corporate Actions
Systematic or low-touch traders need to be alert to anything that may change their positions or affect the price of the securities they are market making on. Getting quality timeline information about corporate actions and stock splits is critical to a trader being able to price securities accurately. Bloomberg offers both a real-time corporate actions feed (CANS) and an end-of-days, file including pre-market activity.
Earnings Calendar
Traders need to be aware when a company is reporting earnings or doing sales calls as there tends to be market volatility after announcements, especially if there is a surprise. Bloomberg offers both a real-time events feed (EVTS) and an end-of-day files containing upcoming events.
Estimates
Understanding the market's expectation of a company's expected performance is critical to understanding the fair price of a security. If it misses expectations at earnings release, systematic traders need to be prepared for market volatility.
Dividend Forecasts
It is important for traders to understand expected ex-dividend dates for securities and sentiment around dividends as this can affect investor demand. Certain types of traders, such as those doing delta one or sec lending, may lend out a security or go short. They need to know expected dividend information to factor this into their premiums, given the dividend payment will be paid to the beneficial owner.
Sanctions
Information can be fed into pre-trade compliance checks to prevent traders trading sanctioned securities.
News
Bloomberg's Events Driven Feed (EDF) spans a number of use-cases including headlines, sentiment and story bodies. Sophisticated algo traders may want to feed this information into their pricing algo to achieve a better execution or more accurate pricing.

Enterprise Data: Credit

Solutions
Value for Clients
New Issue Feed
Traders need to have the new securities set up and mastered in their internal system as quickly as possible so they can trade.
Sustainable Debt
Understand which bonds are green. Can flag use of proceeds in internal systems.
Real-Time Pricing & Volume
Multiple pricing sources such as
  • BVAL, CBBT and MSG1: To understand the price of an instrument.
  • BVAL: For accurate pricing and benchmark comparison with third parties. Especially important for illiquid securities.
  • USP: Wide coverage.
  • CBBT: Shows composite pricing from dealers and is considered timely. Same use-case as BVAL, albeit for more live data vs evaluated.
  • MSG1: Prices sent to the firm by sellside trader runz. These are prices that are being quoted directly to the dealing desk.
  • MIFID/TRACE Data: Reported trade and quote data.
LQA
Primarily used for risk to understand liquidity of securities and market impact.
Default Risk
Understand the default risk of a company and its securities.
