Build the Narrative

Identify trends and client challenges to open doors to sales opportunities. Learn about the competitive landscape, questions to ask and what solutions we can offer.

Context

Interest rates are starting to have an impact on the wider economy. Economic slowdown and predicted mild recession may help lower inflation further. Monetary policy reversal is initiating some rebound. As the US begins to cut rates, there could be potential capital movement from USD to undervalued currencies, causing them to outperform USD. Due to the rate cutting environment and geopolitical events, FX volatility will increase.

Some 50% of the world’s population are facing elections in a polarised political landscape, with >2 billion votes across 50 countries (half the global economy). Political (in)stability is more troubling than it was 12 months ago. The US election is impossible to call, so we can expect volatility.

OTC derivatives reporting is becoming increasingly relevant globally. Locally each industry such as insurance and pensions will have a range of regulatory requirements on asset allocation and performance.

Asset owners like pension funds usually match assets with liabilities. In many countries, the asset base won't cover the liabilities as people live longer and therefore pension benefits need to be paid out longer. Extra risk will need to be put into the portfolios to attempt to achieve a higher return.

Both asset owners and asset managers are increasingly focusing on ESG and ethical investing in many parts of the world. Sustainabilty is being incorporated into investment products, and firms are integrating its principles into the heart of their purpose, strategy and performance management.

Regulation and public pressure are driving factors, resulting in increased transparency and reporting for asset owners. Some U.S. states are trying to limit ESG considerations from investing, while the EU, California and many other parts of the world have increased certain ESG-related disclosure requirements.

Even with the challenges of lower liquidity and access, asset owners are increasing allocations to alternative asset classes such as private equity, private credit or real estate, which have lower correlations with traditional markets. They require asset management solutions that can incorporate these, like PORT.

Connect

2024 is the year of “more”: more elections, more news, more uncertainty. Known client challenges include too much information and too little time, difficulty cutting through noise and generating meaningful insight quickly, consistency of data and putting data into context, and discoverability of content due to fragmentation of technology.

Clients face the challenge of deciding whether falling inflation suggests a “soft landing” for global economies, or a recession; Central Bank pivots will cause volatility as market participants try to anticipate timing of cuts.

And with more elections than ever before in 2024 and outcomes harder to call, customers face difficulty monitoring opinion and modelling outcomes. New risks can accelerate quickly. Finding trusted data and staying close to predictions and opinions is challenging.

The rally in equity markets has not lifted all stocks equally and there are now wide variations in valuations across sectors and regions. Clients will need to know where the big outliers are now and how to dig deeper into specific stocks they are screening.

Credit spreads are currently historically tight, but market sentiment is expecting credit default rates to rise due to the macro environment. Evaluating relative value across sectors and issuers is increasingly important. Sharp moves in government bonds have left some markets distorted. Tracking and explaining these dislocations is also important.

Economists, credit investors and equity investors offer conflicting outlooks for 2024, making equity forecasts particularly difficult. Clients need to understand different perspectives on the economy and their impact on the investable/ tradable universe.

Private asset markets have grown fourfold over the past decade to command $10 trillion, according to data from alternative-asset consultancy Preqin. This presents a challenge for most asset owners as they want to be able to analyze the impact of such investments on a portfolio in terms of cashflow, returns and risk exposures. This requires both data and creating custom securities in a portfolio to track things like real estate and private funds.

Private Equity benchmarking represents a huge challenge for Asset owners, as well. Asset owners do not have many options for PE benchmark selection. Cambridge Associates is well known in this space.
